Multi-Point Tyre Contacts

CSP’s 5-point tyre model delivers far more nuanced transitions over uneven surfaces—EVO still uses a single-patch model.

Cosmic Suspension

EVO refines stock spring/damper behavior but can’t match CSP’s “atomic” joint-based suspension with real-time camber, roll center shifts, anti-dive/squat, etc.

Dynamic Chassis Flex & Torsion

You feel more chassis flex in CSP under high loads; EVO’s improvements are still a set of tuned rigid-body parameters rather than true frame deformation.

Yaw-Aware TC/ABS & On-the-Fly Physics Swaps

CSP lets you switch ECU maps, tyre types and full physics configs live, with traction/ABS algorithms that factor in yaw rate—EVO has none of these mid-session toggles.

Server-Side Collision & Advanced Wear Models

CSP exposes ERP/CFM tuning for custom crash behavior and temperature-based tyre wear curves—EVO sticks to stock AC collision and wear logic.

Yes, AC EVO physics are better than stock Assetto Corsa: noticeably smoother load transfers, more precise tyre and FFB feedback, and generally a more immersive feeling.

No, AC EVO is not “better” than CSP Extended Physics: it lacks the multi-point tyre model, true suspension joints, dynamic chassis flex, and advanced telemetry/physics-swap features that CSP’s paid version brings.

If your goal is the most authentic, tunable simulation possible, CSP + Extended Physics + Cosmic Suspensions still holds the crown.

But for a plug-and-play uplift over vanilla AC, EVO is a solid step up—just not the final word in sim-car realism.
